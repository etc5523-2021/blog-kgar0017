India COVID Stats

Data Description

The Blog is using the data collected by ourworldindata.org and stored and updated timely in their github repo here. The team of Our World in Data has further collected this data from various sources such as:

  • Confirmed Cases: This data comes from the COVID-19 Data Repository by the Center for Systems Science and Engineering (CSSE) at Johns Hopkins University (JHU)
  • Hospitalizations and intensive care unit (ICU) admissions: our data comes from the European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control (ECDC) for a select number of European countries; and government sources for the United Kingdom, the United States, Canada, Israel, Algeria, and Switzerland.
  • Testing for COVID-19: This data is collected by the Our World in Data team from official reports; you can find further details in our post on COVID-19 testing, including our checklist of questions to understand testing data, information on geographical and temporal coverage, and detailed country-by-country source information. The testing dataset is updated around twice a week.
  • Vaccinations against COVID-19: Yhis data is collected by the Our World in Data team from official reports.
  • Other variables: This data is collected from a variety of sources (United Nations, World Bank, Global Burden of Disease, Blavatnik School of Government, etc.). More information is available in the codebook.

The data is available in csv, xlsx, and json format. I wll be working on csv format for this blog post.

The main dataset is of dimensions 112442, 62, whereas that of India alone is 576, 62.

Which month in each year saw maximum reporting of new cases ?

Figure 1: Trend of total cases in a month each year

The above graph depicts following points:

  • In 2020, the increase in covid cases was pretty much stead and saw a gradual increase after the month 5 and reached to its peak in 9th month with a total of 2.621418^{6} alone in this month.
  • The bove point seems to be pretty true also as India was in full lockdown till the end of May 2020, after which we only see a gradual increase in cases.
  • In 2021, cases started increasing in 2nd month, but saw a showcking rise after the 3rd month (in-time, when the 2nd wave of covid kicked in) and reach to its all time high of 9.010075^{6} alone in 5th month, after which t again dropped drastically to almost same to its total in 2nd month.
